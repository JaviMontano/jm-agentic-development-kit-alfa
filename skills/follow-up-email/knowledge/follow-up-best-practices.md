# Follow-Up Email Best Practices

## The 24-Hour Rule

Send follow-up emails within 24 hours of the meeting. After 48 hours, context fades and urgency drops. Ideal timing:

- **Same day (2-4 hours after):** Best for short meetings with clear action items
- **Next morning (before 10 AM):** Best for late-afternoon meetings or complex discussions
- **Never after 48 hours:** If delayed, acknowledge the delay in the email

## Personalization Principles

### What to personalize:
- Greeting with first name (never "Dear Sir/Madam" or "Team")
- Only the recipient's action items (never expose others' tasks)
- Context relevant to their specific tasks
- Tone appropriate to their seniority and relationship

### What to keep consistent:
- Meeting title and date reference
- General next steps shared by all attendees
- Closing and sender signature
- Overall professional tone

## Action Item Clarity

Every action item should answer three questions:

1. **What** — Specific, measurable task description
2. **When** — Concrete deadline (date, not "soon" or "ASAP")
3. **Why** — Brief context connecting the task to a meeting decision

### Examples of Good vs. Poor Action Items

| Poor | Good |
|------|------|
| "Handle the marketing stuff" | "Prepare campaign brief including budget breakdown and target audience" |
| "Fix the bug" | "Resolve login timeout issue reported in ticket #234" |
| "ASAP" | "By April 8, 2026 (before Q2 kickoff)" |
| "Look into it" | "Research three vendor options and present comparison by Friday" |

## Reminder Scheduling

### When to create calendar reminders:

- **Deadlines more than 3 days away:** Set reminder for 24 hours before deadline
- **Deadlines within 3 days:** Set reminder for morning of deadline day
- **Multi-step tasks:** Set intermediate reminder at midpoint
- **Team dependencies:** Remind the upstream person 48h before the downstream person's deadline

### Reminder event format:

- Title: `[Pendiente] Brief task description`
- Description: Full action item with context and link to meeting notes
- Duration: 30-minute block (for review/completion time)
- Invitee: The responsible person only

## Batch Sending Strategy

For meetings with many attendees:

1. **Draft all emails first** — never auto-send
2. **Present summary table** showing recipient, item count, earliest deadline
3. **Allow per-email edits** before sending
4. **Send with 15-30 second intervals** to avoid rate limiting
5. **Confirm delivery** and report any bounces

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **CC'ing everyone:** Each person should get their own email with only their items
2. **Generic templates:** The email should feel personally written, not mass-produced
3. **Missing context:** Don't assume the recipient remembers every meeting detail
4. **No deadline:** Every action item needs a concrete date
5. **Over-formatting:** Keep it simple; complex HTML may break in some email clients
6. **Forgetting the "why":** Link each task to the meeting decision that created it
7. **Ignoring language:** Match the language of the meeting and recipient preference
