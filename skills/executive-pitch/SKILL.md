# Executive Pitch

> "If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough." — Albert Einstein

## TL;DR

Constructs C-level business cases with ROI projections, cost-of-inaction analysis, and executive-ready narratives that translate technical value into business outcomes. Use this skill when seeking budget approval, presenting to leadership, or justifying technology investments.

## Procedure

### Step 1: Discover
- Identify the audience: CEO, CTO, CFO, board — each cares about different metrics
- Gather business context: strategic priorities, pain points, competitive pressures
- Collect data: current costs, efficiency metrics, market benchmarks, competitor moves

### Step 2: Analyze
- Build the ROI model: investment required vs. value delivered over 1-3 years
- Calculate cost-of-inaction: what happens if we do nothing (market share loss, technical debt compound interest, compliance risk)
- Identify strategic alignment: how this initiative supports stated business objectives
- Prepare objection responses for common executive concerns

### Step 3: Execute
- Create a one-page executive summary with the ask, the why, and the impact
- Produce an ROI breakdown: investment timeline, payback period, NPV
- Write cost-of-inaction scenario with quantified business risk
- Design a presentation deck (5-7 slides max) following the Pyramid Principle
- Include a clear ask: budget, timeline, decision needed, next steps

### Step 4: Validate
- Verify financial projections use conservative, defensible assumptions
- Confirm the narrative answers "so what?" for every claim
- Check that technical details are translated into business outcomes
- Review with a trusted advisor before presenting to executives

## Quality Criteria

- [ ] ROI uses conservative assumptions with sensitivity ranges
- [ ] Cost-of-inaction is quantified, not just qualitative
- [ ] Presentation follows Pyramid Principle (conclusion first)
- [ ] Clear ask with specific budget, timeline, and decision required
- [ ] Evidence tags applied to all claims

## Anti-Patterns

- Leading with technology instead of business outcomes
- Overpromising ROI with best-case-only projections
- Presenting without a clear ask or decision point
